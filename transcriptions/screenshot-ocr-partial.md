## Partial HR FAQ OCR findings

The partial OCR matches the RCM resource packet on these points:

- The document is titled **Revenue Cycle Transformation FAQs**.
- The redesigned operating model is scheduled for implementation between **June 1 and Aug. 23, 2026**.
- Included entities are clinical revenue creation work at **JHU School of Medicine**, **JHHS**, and **Johns Hopkins Community Physicians**.
- The employee's employer will officially move from **SOM** to **JHHS**.
- Current SOM positions are being eliminated.
- Employees may either be mapped to a role through **administrative transfer** or go through **competitive recruitment**.
- For administrative transfers, declining the mapped role means no severance and employment ends on the notification date.
- If retirement-eligible, the employee cannot both move into the new role and retire during the transition; they must choose one path for the transition.

  - training and administrative support roles

  - director and assistant director positions
  - manager and supervisor positions
  - billing and follow-up roles
  - coding educator roles and clinical denial specialist/coding positions
- Application deadlines shown:
  - **June 12** for administrative transfers and management positions
  - **June 19** for billing and AR follow-up
- Administrative-transfer employees are contacted for a placement meeting and then receive an offer letter.
- Offer letters must be signed and returned electronically within **three business days**.
- Internal candidates are prioritized; outside hiring is only for positions not filled internally.
- No relocation is required.
- Work environment expectations should be consistent with the current role unless changes are clearly communicated.

## Q&A 6/5/26 spreadsheet findings

Source: `screenshots/Q&A_6_5_26/`

The spreadsheet was restricted: the UI says there is no permission to download or print the file. Findings below are transcribed/summarized from downscaled screenshots.

### 403(b)

- JHU 403(b) contributions stop at the final paycheck.
- JHU employer contributions continue through the last full month.
- Rollover/distribution options are available after termination.
- JHHS provides a 403(b) match after transition.
- The screenshot describes the JHHS match as **4% + 2% match**.
- Withdrawal before age **59 1/2** may trigger an IRS penalty.

### Application process

- Employees can apply for multiple roles.
- Internal candidates are prioritized through an internal-first hiring approach.
- Application deadlines depend on role: **June 12** and **June 19**.
- Interviews are required for competitive roles, not for administrative-transfer roles.
- Interview decisions depend on the interview and selection process; notifications are provided when evaluations are complete.
- Interviews are virtual.
- Panel interviews use leaders from multiple departments and standardized criteria.
- Employees cannot target specific departments when applying, but can share preferences during the interview process.
- Pay grades are reflected on job descriptions.
- Jobs are primarily internal; external posting occurs only if roles are unfilled.
- Additional postings are not expected for the PBS team at that time, but employees are encouraged to keep checking for openings.

### Benefits and float holidays

- Benefits change after transition because employees transition to JHHS plans.
- PTO accrual level carries over based on transferred service years.
- JHHS does **not** offer daycare vouchers, but has a day care vendor, Bright Horizons.
- JHHS recognizes spouses for domestic partner/spouse-related benefits. Confirm exact dependent/domestic partner rules with JHHS benefits.
- JHU float holidays become available July 1 and can be used with manager discretion/approval, but expire and are **not paid out** as of transfer to JHHS or termination from JHU.

### Communication

- For HR questions, use HR Support Center or listed HR contacts.
- If materials were not received by email, contact Amy Mantegna.
- The PowerPoint was emailed and also available through the resource hub link.
- Slides were expected to be available in the resource hub for download.
- The meeting was not recorded; additional sessions would be provided.

### Compensation

- Salary changes depend on role.
- Administrative transfers will not reduce salary.
- Administrative transfers are protected from pay reductions.
- Job descriptions with corresponding pay grades are available in the resource hub.
- Pay schedule/frequency changes to **bi-weekly**.

### Employment transition

- Transition timing is **June 1-August 23, 2026**.
- One answer says the **August 23 transition date** applies, and RIF employees may extend into early September.
- Another answer says the final day of employment is **August 22** because August 23 is a Sunday. This should be clarified in the individual notice/offer letter.
- Current SOM role is eliminated; transition occurs by transfer or recruitment.
- If an employee does not get a job, HR guides next steps including severance if eligible.
- Employees are not considered new employees for service-time purposes; service time carries over.
- If an employee declines an administrative-transfer role, there is no severance.
- If the employee does not move forward, they should notify their manager and HR representative; final working day is confirmed based on the situation.
- There is a **90-day probationary period**.
- Failing probation affects severance eligibility; the answer indicates loss of severance option if the JHHS position was accepted.
- Transferred equipment may be transitioned or reissued as needed; details are shared during onboarding.

### Health benefits

- Medical, dental, and vision coverage continues through the end of the month.
- COBRA is available at **102%** of the full group rate.
- One answer says there should be no health-insurance gap because coverage continues through the end of the month, for example through **8/31**.
- JHHS health insurance begins on the **first day of the month after hire**; employees have **30 days** to enroll.
- COBRA packet/details are sent with continuation information.
- COBRA rate information was linked to the JHU benefits page and a 2026 COBRA rates PDF.
- If someone is in the group without placement and receives a final RIF notice, there may be severance funds intended to subsidize COBRA short-term. The employee remains responsible for electing COBRA and submitting payments.
- FSA contributions stop; claims are allowed up to **120 days**; unused funds are forfeited.
- HSA balance is retained; contributions stop; fees may apply.
- Life insurance and AD&D coverage continue to month-end and may be converted within **31 days**.
- Dental/vision do not convert like life insurance; staff moving to JHHS need to elect JHHS benefits because these do not transfer.
- Payroll deductions such as credit union deductions are not expected to be an issue.

### Pension / retirement

- One pension answer says service credit stops, but vested employees are eligible for benefits at retirement age.
- Another pension answer says the pension plan is closed and all JHU rehired and transitioning employees enter the JHHS 403(b) match plan, with immediate vesting for those with at least one year of service and 1,000+ hours worked with JHU.
- Another pension answer says the **JHHS pension plan is closed as of 1/1/2026**, and current pension-eligible employees will remain pension eligible.
- Retirement information sessions are referenced for **June 10** and **June 16**.
- Employees considering retirement should contact Amy Mantegna or HR as soon as a decision is made and before **August 22**.
- If an employee chooses to retire, final employment date varies and should be confirmed with Amy Mantegna.
- Additional retirement information was expected during retirement sessions on June 10 and June 16.
- One row says employees impacted by the transition who retire are eligible to receive severance benefits.

Important caveat: The pension answers appear to discuss multiple scenarios and possibly different plans. They should not be treated as a final individual pension determination. Get written confirmation from Benefits/HR for the employee's exact JHU pension status, accrued benefit, whether accrual freezes, and what JHHS retirement plan applies.

### PTO / leave

- For vacation/PTO, the spreadsheet repeats the JHU vacation payout cap language:
  - hired/reinstated on or after **July 1, 2013**: payout of accrued unused vacation up to **22 days**.
  - hired/reinstated before **July 1, 2013**: payout of accrued unused vacation up to **44 days**.
  - vacation above the limit is forfeited at termination/retirement.
- In response to whether vacation transfers or is paid out when transitioning to JHHS, the same payout-cap language is repeated.
- Sick leave: one Q&A answer says no payout unless qualified as a retiree; may be reinstated if rehired within six months.
- One Q&A answer says "PTO (22 days)" is combined vacation and sick time.
- PTO reset at JHHS: PTO is paid out, and new accrual is based on recognized years of service.
- Retiring sick leave payout: staff retiring after 30+ years of continuous full-time University service, or at age 55+ with at least 10 years of continuous full-time University service immediately before retirement, are paid unused sick leave in excess of 45 days at retirement.
- Service time: years of service fully carry over for benefits, PTO, and eligibility.
- Breaks longer than one year may not count.
- A later row asks why accrued sick leave balances are not transferred to JHHS and points to the JHU-JHHS Administrative Transfer Guidelines PDF for details.

Important caveat: The Q&A sick-leave items are potentially in tension with HR025/HR031, which say sick leave transfers in an administrative transfer. This should be clarified in writing with HR: does the employee's actual sick leave balance transfer to JHHS, and if not, what policy overrides HR025/HR031?

### Severance

- Severance is available if not placed through competitive recruitment, but not if declining an administrative transfer.
- Severance is capped at **26 weeks** for 26+ years of service.
- If tenure is less than one year, one answer says one week of pay for a partial year of more than six months full-time JHU employment, with a minimum of **4 weeks** paid.
- Severance uses current years of service under JHU policy.
- Severance is calculated in weeks per year.
- Severance is paid as a **lump sum**.
- Severance is processed after termination date and completed signatures on the severance agreement.
- Unemployment outcomes are determined by the state.
- Competitive-recruitment employees may remain severance-eligible if they withdraw an application.
- Competitive-placement employees who decline a position offer due to pay may still be severance-eligible.
- For administrative-transfer scenarios, declining an offer due to pay does **not** preserve severance.
- Employees who retire may still receive severance in some transition scenarios, according to one row, but this needs individual confirmation.
- RIF notification letters are expected in **mid-August**.

### Systems/access, tuition, remote work

- Email access continues during the transition period. If placed into a new role, access continues; if employment ends, email access ends after termination.
- Current tuition reimbursement semester is honored; future JHU eligibility ends.
- Dependent tuition mid-program may continue if the prior entity provides a letter with eligibility dates for JHHS.
- Dependent tuition eligibility depends on service recognition.
- JHHS offers tuition assistance; see benefits presentation for details.
- Revenue cycle roles are expected to remain mostly remote; some positions are hybrid or onsite and continue their current status.
- Flexible schedule options exist within revenue cycle.
- The organization says it created significant opportunities in revenue cycle, but competitive-role placement is not guaranteed.
- Remote/residency eligibility is based on current residency requirements, and impacted staff were identified as eligible for RCM positions.
