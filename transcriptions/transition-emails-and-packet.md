## RCM resource packet

Source: `Resource Packet All Other Affected Staff (1).pdf` inside `attachments/Revenue Cycle Transition.zip`

Extraction status: text extracted locally.

Key findings:

- The redesigned operating model is scheduled for implementation between **June 1 and Aug. 23, 2026**.
- The transformation includes revenue cycle work at **JHU School of Medicine**, **JHHS**, and **Johns Hopkins Community Physicians**.
- The packet says the employee's employer will officially move from **SOM/JHU** to **JHHS**.
- Current SOM positions are being eliminated as part of the transition.
- Employees may either be mapped to a JHHS role by **administrative transfer** or go through **competitive recruitment**, depending on role.
- The packet lists **coding** as a role group being mapped by **administrative transfer**.
- For administrative transfers, the employee submits an application but does **not** need to interview; the application formalizes the transition.
- Administrative-transfer employees should receive a placement meeting and then an official offer letter.
- Offer letters must be signed and returned electronically within **three business days**.
- Administrative-transfer application deadline: **June 12, 2026**.
- If an employee is offered a mapped administrative-transfer role and declines it, the packet says the employee is **not eligible for severance** and **not eligible for outplacement services**.
- For administrative transfers, the packet says the employee will be **kept whole**, meaning salary will **not decrease**.
- Payroll will move to the new employer, employees will use **JHWorks**, and new tax forms will be required.
- Employees should check the first JHHS paycheck carefully.
- Benefits will transition to the new employer's plan.
- Health insurance enrollment must be submitted within **30 days from the hire date**.
- Health coverage starts on the **first day of the month after the hire date**.
- Continuous Johns Hopkins service counts for benefits calculations, including **PTO accruals, retirement vesting, 403(b) employer match, FMLA, Paid Parental Leave, and dependent tuition benefits**, subject to plan terms and conditions.
- The packet says **JHU will pay out 100% of unused accrued vacation/PTO when the transfer happens**, according to JHU policies.
- Vacation/PTO accrual rates at the new organization will be based on the employee's JHU employment date.
- JHHS new-hire orientation is required.
- The packet says all employees have a **90-day probationary period** as part of the administrative transfer process, even though Johns Hopkins service is recognized for benefits purposes.
- A JHU retirement information session was scheduled for **June 16 at 11 a.m.**, with follow-up one-on-one benefits specialist options.

### Q&A 6/5/26 spreadsheet

Source: `onedrive_docs/RCM Transition Communications/HR FAQs/Q&A_ 6_5_26.xlsx`

This downloaded spreadsheet supersedes the earlier screenshot-only review of the Q&A.

Key findings:

- JHU 403(b) contributions stop at the final paycheck, and JHU employer contributions continue through the last full month of employment.
- The Q&A says JHHS provides a 403(b) match after transition, described as **4% + 2% match**, subject to eligibility and vesting requirements.
- The Q&A says PTO accrual level carries over based on transferred service years.
- The Q&A says JHU float holidays become available July 1, can be used with manager discretion/approval, but expire and are **not paid out** at transfer to JHHS or JHU termination.
- Salary for administrative transfers should not be reduced; the Q&A says administrative transfers are protected from pay reductions.
- Pay frequency changes to **bi-weekly**.
- Transition timing is listed as **June 1-Aug. 23, 2026**.
- One row says the final day of employment is **August 22** because the August 23 transition date falls on a Sunday. Confirm the individual date in the offer/notice letter.
- Employees are not considered new employees for service-time purposes; service time carries over.
- There is a **90-day probationary period**.
- Medical, dental, and vision coverage continue through the end of the month; one answer says there should be no gap because coverage continues through the end of the month, for example through **8/31**.
- JHHS health insurance starts on the first day of the month after hire, with **30 days** to enroll.
- COBRA is available at **102%** of the full group rate.
- FSA contributions stop; claims are allowed up to **120 days**; unused funds are forfeited.
- HSA balance is retained; contributions stop; fees may apply.
- Life insurance and AD&D continue to month-end and may be converted within **31 days**.
- Dental and vision do not convert like life insurance; staff moving to JHHS need to elect JHHS benefits because these do not transfer automatically.
- Pension answers are mixed and need individual confirmation. The Q&A says service credit stops and vested employees remain eligible for benefits at retirement age. It also says the pension plan is closed and transitioning employees enter the JHHS 403(b) match plan, with immediate vesting for those with at least one year of service and 1,000+ hours worked with JHU. A later row says the JHHS pension plan is closed as of **1/1/2026** and current pension-eligible employees remain pension eligible.
- Vacation/PTO answers repeat the JHU payout cap: hires/reinstatements before **July 1, 2013** can receive payout for accrued unused vacation up to **44 days**; hires/reinstatements on or after that date can receive up to **22 days**.
- The Q&A says PTO is paid out, and new JHHS accrual is based on recognized years of service.
- Sick-leave answers point to the administrative-transfer guidelines. The later-pulled administrative-transfer FAQ says sick time balances are not paid out and do not transfer, even though HR025/HR031 contain conflicting sick-transfer wording. Treat this as a document conflict to clarify in writing, not a settled answer.
- Service time is described as fully carrying over for benefits, PTO, and eligibility.
- RIF notification letters are expected in **mid-August**.
- Remote revenue-cycle roles are expected to remain mostly remote; hybrid/onsite positions continue current status.

### JHHS benefits presentation

Source: `onedrive_docs/RCM Transition Communications/JHHS Benefits/2026 Updated Benefits Presentation 6_3_26.pdf`

Key findings:

- Employees regularly scheduled to work **20 or more hours per week** are benefits-eligible.
- Benefit enrollment must be completed within **30 days**.
- JHHS medical options listed are EHP Direct Primary Care, PPO, and EPO.
- Dental is through Delta Dental; vision is through Superior Vision.
- FSA contributions are biweekly through payroll deductions.
- The presentation lists **7 holidays**.
- PTO accrual tables differ for non-exempt and exempt employees. If 18 years of service is recognized and the employee falls in the **10-19 years** row:
  - Non-exempt: **8.31 hours per pay period**, annual maximum **216 hours / 27 days**.
  - Exempt: **8.62 hours per pay period**, annual maximum **224 hours / 28 days**.
- The **20+ years** row is **9.85 hours per pay period**, annual maximum **256 hours / 32 days**, for both non-exempt and exempt employees.
- The actual JHHS PTO accrual rate still needs individual confirmation because it depends on recognized service date and exempt/non-exempt status.
- Short-term disability is described as company-paid, with a 7-calendar-day elimination period and 60% of pay for up to 25 weeks.
- Long-term disability is described as employee-paid unless waived, 60% of salary after 26 weeks, up to a monthly maximum.
- Employee tuition assistance is described as eligible for classes beginning after **90 days of employment**.
- Dependent child tuition assistance is described as available after **4 years of continuous full-time employment**, with additional hour and plan conditions.

### JHU benefits summary - termination

Source: `onedrive_docs/RCM Transition Communications/JHHS Benefits/Benefits-Summary_Termination.pdf`

Document date shown: **12/2025**

This is a general JHU termination summary. It is relevant background, but it may not override the more specific JHU/JHHS administrative-transfer policies.

Key findings:

- Medical, pharmacy, dental, and vision coverage continue until the end of the month in which employment ends.
- COBRA is available at **102%** of the full group rate.
- Life insurance and AD&D continue until the end of the month and may be converted within **31 days** of termination.
- 403(b) contributions stop with the last pay received; University contribution is made through the last full month of work.
- Support Staff Pension service credits stop on the termination date; vested employees are eligible to receive plan benefits as early as age **55**.
- FSA contributions stop with the last pay received; eligible expenses through the last day of employment can be submitted, with claims allowed up to **120 days** after termination.
- HSA balance stays with the employee, while contributions stop and fees may apply.
- Vacation remaining balance is paid to eligible full/part-time support staff and senior staff who terminate after 120 days of employment.
- Sick leave has no payout unless the employee qualifies as a retiree; eligible rehired staff may be credited with unused sick leave if rehired within the stated timeframe and criteria.
