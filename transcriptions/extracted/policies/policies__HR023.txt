Policy Statement
Johns Hopkins University is committed to equal opportunity for its faculty, staff and students.  The University is committed
to providing qualified individuals access to all academic and employment programs, benefits and activities on the basis of
demonstrated ability, performance and merit without regard to personal factors that are irrelevant to the program involved. The
Human Resources Offices are responsible for all recruiting activity and for approving all employment commitments including
salary, position classification and any special conditions of employment for staff. Faculty position descriptions should be
submitted to the department chair or designee for review and final approval.
Who Is Governed By This Policy
All non-represented University faculty and staff.
Exceptions/Exclusions
Students, post-doctoral fellows, and employees of the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory.
Policy Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define positions (employee groups) that are categorized by hours worked, the regularity of the
work schedule, and the duration of the position.

Definitions
Casual (As Needed) A position where employees are not expected to work more than 27 hours per week.
  These employees may have a regular/consistent, infrequent or variable schedule and
may work as needed throughout the year.  They may not work more than 987 hours
in a 12-month period.  Faculty may not be classified as casual employees.
Casual (Seasonal)
A position where employees are hired to satisfy or meet the needs of a department in
a cyclical and recurring nature.  Seasonal work is short term (lasting no more than six
(6)  months) and recurs around the same time each year.  Seasonal employees may
have a regular/consistent, infrequent or variable schedule.  They may not work more
than 987 hours in a six (6) month period.  Employees who work other assignments
throughout the year cannot be considered seasonal employees.
Full-time
A regular position planned to last longer than six (6) months that requires a minimum
of 28 hours per week to be worked on a regularly scheduled basis or an actual salary
of 75% or more of the full-time equivalent salary.
Limited
A regular position planned to last no more than six (6) months and averages 28 hours or more
per week, which cannot exceed 987 hours in a six (6) month period.
A regular position planned to last more than six (6) months and averages between 18 and 27
hours per week, which cannot exceed 987 hours in a twelve (12) month period.
All faculty positions that are not classified as full or part time are considered limited.
Part-time
A regular position planned to last longer than six (6) months that requires fewer
than 28 hours per week, but at least 19 hours per week, to be worked on a regularly
scheduled basis.  The actual annual salary for a part time faculty position must be
less than 75% but at least 51% of the full-time equivalent annual salary.

Policy
Position categories define the University's standard employment groups and should be maintained for all University staff and
faculty positions.  For staff positions, supervisors should maintain a current position description for every position under their
supervision and purview that reflects the department's current needs.  This documentation enables all parties to understand the
position's duties and responsibilities of a position and should be used as a tool in performance reviews and appraisals.  Faculty
position descriptions should accurately reflect the qualifications and area of specialization(s) and not be unduly restrictive.
This documentation should be developed and reviewed in collaboration with the department chair and any other appropriate
individuals, then it should be approved by the department chair or his/her designee.

II.
Working Hours Threshold
An employee in a casual position who has reached the 987 working hour threshold may only be hired or
employed in benefits-eligible assignments at the University.  If an employee is anticipated to work beyond
987 hours for all assignments within the University, please consult with a representative from Central Human
Resources to determine whether the employee should be converted to full-time, part-time or limited staff.
Casual staff who reach this threshold may not continue to work until they are converted to a benefits-eligible
status or when their hours fall below the 987 working hours threshold.  In order to determine when the working
hours threshold has been met, use the employee's hire date and look back 12 months.  Following the first
year of employment, the measurement period begins with October 15 of the current year to October 15 of the
previous year.  If Central Human Resources and the department agree to change the classification, neither the
employee group nor benefits eligibility will be made retroactively.

III. Nine (9) or Ten (10) Month Academic Year Positions
A nine (9) or ten (10) month academic year position ("Academic Year Position") is one that is for a defined
period or, alternatively, for the fall and spring semesters with a break in between semesters. Academic Year
Positions may be used, for example, for staff positions that support academic year faculty appointments or if a
department has fluctuating demands.

A.
Eligibility
Departments should decide whether an Academic Year Position meets their strategic, financial
or operational needs.  If so, then the department should determine whether to convert an existing twelve
(12) month position to a nine (9) or (10) ten month Academic Year Position, with reasonable and
appropriate notice to any incumbents.

B.
Position Scope and Structure
Employees in Academic Year Positions typically work nine (9) or ten (10) months and are placed on leave
without pay status for the three or two non-working months period.  The incumbent’s standard hours will
remain the same during the entire year (inclusive of working months and non-working months). The SAP
reason code for the leave of absence without pay will be "Position Schedule".

C.
Base Salary and Annual Review
Base pay of employees in Academic Year Positions will be pro-rated and paid only during the working
months.  Annual performance and salary review will be as scheduled and annual salary adjustments will
be calculated based on the incumbent’s academic year salary.

D.
Work Commitment During Leave of Absence

Policy Enforcement
Violations
It is a violation of this policy not to place employees in the proper category based on the
policy definitions above.
Enforcement
The circumstances of any alleged misclassification should be investigated by the
Office of Human Resources or the Office of Hopkins Internal Audits.  Based on the
investigation, HR or OHIA may recommend appropriate follow up, including, but
not limited to, reclassifying employees and disciplinary action up to and including
termination.
Reporting
Violations
Suspected violations may be reported to the Office of Human Resources or via the
Speak2US Compliance Hotline.
