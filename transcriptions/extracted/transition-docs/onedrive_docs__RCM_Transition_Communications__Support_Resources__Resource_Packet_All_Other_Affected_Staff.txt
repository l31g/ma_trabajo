Revenue Cycle Transformation FAQs

We hope these FAQs will answer questions you may have during the transformation.

Reasoning Behind the Transformation

1. Why is this transformation happening?

In early 2025, Revenue Cycle leadership took a close look at how we work — our
structure, our processes and how well we’re integrated across the system. The goal
was to identify opportunities to strengthen our performance and align with industry
best practices.

--- page 2 ---
2

What we learned pointed to some clear next steps, starting with updating our
organizational structure under Johns Hopkins Health System (JHHS). This redesign will
help us work more efficiently by streamlining workﬂows, improving visibility into our
operations, and making it easier to make informed decisions quickly.

At the same time, moving to an enterprise-level structure under JHHS means we’ll
have better coordination across teams, more consistent standards, shared resources
and aligned goals systemwide. These changes directly support Johns Hopkins’
mission to improve the health of our community and advance medical knowledge —
work that depends on a strong revenue cycle foundation. When we operate more
effectively behind the scenes, our clinical teams have what they need to focus on
what matters most: delivering exceptional patient care. Ultimately, these changes
position us to better support Johns Hopkins Medicine through improved performance,
clearer reporting and smarter use of our resources.

2. When is this transformation happening?

The redesigned operating model is scheduled for implementation between June 1
and Aug. 23, 2026.

3. Which entities are included in this transformation?

All clinical revenue creation — both hospital billing and professional billing — at the
Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine, Johns Hopkins Health System and
Johns Hopkins Community Physicians is included.

4. What’s changing — and what’s staying the same?

Here’s what’s changing:

• Your employer. You’ll officially move from the school of medicine (SOM) to
Johns Hopkins Health System (JHHS). Beneﬁts information can be found
below.
• Leadership structure. We’re bringing all of Revenue Cycle under one leader to
improve coordination. If you previously worked under SOM, you’ll have a
designated liaison who will be your main point of contact.
• How we work. We’re adjusting some processes to improve efficiency and our
collections.

Here’s what’s NOT changing:

• Your mission. You’ll continue supporting our patients and keeping revenue
operations running smoothly.

• Our commitment to you. We are dedicated to your professional growth and
personal well-being.
• Your daily work. The essential work you do every day remains just as important
as it has always been.
Employment Impact and Transition

1. Who will be affected by this transformation?

All roles within the SOM revenue cycle organization will be affected by this change.

2. What happens now that my current position is being eliminated?

With the transition from SOM to JHHS, your current SOM position is being eliminated.
Depending on your role, you could be invited to competitively apply to future state
roles with JHHS (“competitive recruitment”) or be mapped to a role from SOM to JHHS
via an administrative transfer (“administrative transfer”). Learn more in the next
section: Application Process and Future Roles.

3. What happens if I do not secure another position within the system?

o For administrative transfers: If you are offered a mapped role and choose to
decline it, you will not be eligible for severance. Your employment will end on the
date speciﬁed in your notiﬁcation.

o For competitive recruitments: If you do not apply for or are not selected as a
result of a competitive recruitment, and you are unable to secure another role
within the Johns Hopkins enterprise before your current position ends, HR will
guide you through next steps, which will include severance, outplacement services
and other resources to assist with your career transition.

4. Will I receive a severance package?

o For administrative transfers: If you are offered a mapped role and choose to
decline it, you will not be eligible for a severance package.

o For competitive recruitments: If you do not apply for, or are not selected, as a
result of a competitive recruitment, you will be eligible for severance. If applicable,
SOM HR will provide speciﬁc information regarding severance packages, including
any continuation of pay and beneﬁts.

5. If I am retirement-eligible, can I retire from the university and then apply for one
of the new RCM positions?

--- page 4 ---
4

During the transition, an employee cannot do both. The employee would either move
over to a new role through a competitive recruitment or administrative transfer or
could elect to retire (if eligible). Nothing would prevent an employee from applying for
a future opening.

6. If I am retirement-eligible and choose to retire, can I receive a severance package
without applying for a new role?

• For those under the competitive recruitment category, if you don’t apply
and/or are not selected, you would receive a severance. Likewise, if you elect
to retire, you will receive a severance package.

• For administrative transfers: If you are offered a mapped role and choose to
decline it, you will not be eligible for a severance package, even though you can
choose to retire.

7. What outplacement services are offered?

If you receive a severance package, you’ll have access to an outplacement ﬁrm to
support you through this transition and help with your job search. This resource is
designed to give you practical support as you navigate your next steps.

Please note: If you’re offered an administrative transfer and choose to decline it, you
will not be eligible for outplacement services.

8. Would I be eligible for rehire at Johns Hopkins in the future?

Yes. If you leave in good standing, you are eligible for rehire and welcome to apply for
future openings across Johns Hopkins.

Application Process and Future Roles

1. How does the transition to the JHHS employment model work?

The process depends on your current role and how it transitions to the new Revenue
Cycle organization:

If your role maps directly to a new position via an administrative transfer:

Your role will be mapped to a role at JHHS. You’ll receive a personalized email with a
link to provide your information for your new position. No interview is required — this
link simply allows us to gather the details needed to complete your transfer to JHHS.
Once you submit your information, we’ll schedule a brief placement meeting to

--- page 5 ---
5

answer questions and conﬁrm details. You’ll then receive an official offer letter to
review and accept.


If your role requires a competitive recruitment:

Leadership and certain specialized positions (directors, assistant directors,
managers, supervisors, and billing and follow-up roles) will go through a structured,
competitive interview process. You’ll apply through SuccessFactors and participate in
interviews where we’ll assess your experience, skills and ﬁt for the role.

If you want to explore other opportunities:

You’re welcome to apply for any open position in the new Revenue Cycle organization
or across the Johns Hopkins enterprise. You can apply for multiple roles — we
encourage you to explore options that match your interests and qualiﬁcations.

2. Will I have to interview for a position?

It depends on your role:

Roles requiring a competitive application and interview process:

o All director and assistant director positions
o All manager and supervisor positions
o Billing and follow-up roles
o Coding educator roles and clinical denial specialists (coding positions)

These positions will go through a competitive interview process where candidates are
evaluated based on background, experience, and skills.

Roles being mapped (via administrative transfer):

o coding
o core cash posting
o core refunds
o ﬁnancial clearance
o performance improvement (non-manager roles)
o core operations
o training and administrative support roles

For these positions, if your current role maps to the new organization, you’ll submit an
application but won’t need to interview. This is simply to formalize your transition.

Important Note About Interviews:
The interview process is designed to match current team members with the roles that
best ﬁt their skills and experience within the new organization structure. This is about
ﬁnding the right ﬁt for everyone during the transition — not about competing against
external candidates.

3. When and how do I apply?

Application deadlines by role type:

You’ll receive a list of openings with links to the JHHS application system in
SuccessFactors. Submit your application by the dates below:

o June 12: All administrative transfers and management positions
o June 19: Billing and AR follow-up

After you apply:

o Competitive recruitments: You’ll be contacted to schedule interviews. If
selected, you’ll receive a formal offer letter.
o Administrative transfers: You’ll be contacted for a placement meeting and
then receive an offer letter.

All offer letters must be signed and returned electronically within three business
days. Once accepted, our pre-boarding team will reach out with next steps.

4. Will internal candidates be prioritized over external applicants?

This is an internal process. We’ll only hire outside if we have positions that are not
ﬁlled.

6. Will I have to relocate for this new position?

No, you will not be required to relocate.

7. What are the expectations for the work environment regarding in-person, hybrid
or remote work?

Work environment expectations will be consistent with your current role, or will be
clearly communicated if changes are needed. Details will be provided during the
transition process.

8. Will there be opportunities for growth and advancement?

Absolutely. We’re committed to helping you build your career within the new Revenue
Cycle organization. Here’s how we’ll support your growth:

• Clear paths forward. You’ll be able to see what it takes to move up — the skills
you need, the experience that matters and what strong performance looks like at
each level.
• Leadership support. If you’re interested in moving into supervisory or
management roles, we’ll offer development programs and coaching to help you
get there.
• Hands-on learning. You’ll have chances to stretch yourself through special
projects—like process improvements, system optimization work, or cross-
functional teams—that help you gain new skills and get noticed.
• Training and credentials. We’ll support formal training and certiﬁcations in areas
like revenue cycle ﬁnance, analytics, compliance and operational excellence.
• Ongoing conversations. Your manager will work with you regularly to set goals,
track your progress, and map out your next steps through personalized
development plans.
• Mentorship and connections. We’ll help you connect with senior colleagues and
leaders who can offer guidance, advice and advocacy for your career.
• First look at new roles. When positions open up, current employees will get
priority consideration — so you’ll have the inside track on new opportunities.

Bottom line: We want to help you grow your skills, take on more responsibility and
advance your career right here.

Compensation, Payroll and Beneﬁts

Important!

8

The employee administrative transfer guidelines between The Johns Hopkins University
and Johns Hopkins Health System — which are two distinct organizations — have been
expanded to provide more ﬂexibility and continuity for employees as they grow their
careers at Johns Hopkins. The guidelines cover beneﬁts eligibility, conditions of the
transfer, service dates and more.

Learn more:
• JHU-JHHS administrative guidelines
• JHU-JHHS administrative guidelines FAQs

1. How will this affect my salary?

Each position is compensated at competitive fair market value based on current
market rates. Any salary changes depend on the level of the position you move into,
and will be discussed with you during the transition process. If you’re being transferred
to an administrative role, you will be kept whole — meaning your salary will not
decrease.

2. What payroll changes should I expect?

Once your employer transitions from SOM to JHHS, you’ll notice a few changes to how
you get paid:

•
Your paychecks will come from your new employer. If you still receive paper
checks, they’ll be mailed from the new location, which could take a bit longer.
That’s why we encourage you to sign up for direct deposit — it’s faster and more
reliable.
•
You’ll be using a new payroll system called JHWorks. Don’t worry, it’s
straightforward once you get used to it.
•
Please double-check your ﬁrst paycheck. We know it’s easy to overlook, but
it’s important to make sure everything is correct — better to catch any issues
early on.
•
You’ll need to ﬁll out new tax forms. This includes federal and state forms, so
you’ll want to set aside a few minutes to complete those.

If you have any questions or run into any hiccups along the way, don’t hesitate to reach
out!

3. How will my beneﬁts be affected?

Your beneﬁts will transition to your new employer’s plan, which means there may
be some changes to things like health insurance, life and disability coverage, and your
403(b) retirement plan. We know this can feel like a lot, but we’ll share details as soon

--- page 9 ---
9

as everything is ﬁnalized. In the meantime, you can check out the beneﬁts information
at jhhsbeneﬁts.com (for JHHS) or your new employer’s beneﬁts portal.



Here’s what you need to know:

• Health insurance enrollment: You’ll have up to 30 days from your hire date to
submit your health insurance enrollment. Your coverage will start on the ﬁrst
day of the month after your hire date.
• Your service time counts! Good news — we’re recognizing all your continuous
years of service with Johns Hopkins when calculating your beneﬁts. This
includes things like PTO accruals, retirement vesting, the 403(b) employer
match, FMLA, Paid Parental Leave, and eligibility for dependent tuition
beneﬁts. If you’ve had a break in service of less than a year, that time still
counts. However, if the break was longer than a year, we won’t be able to credit
that prior service (subject to plan terms and conditions).
• Unused vacation payout: JHU will pay out any unused, accrued vacation or
PTO you have according to their policies — 100% of your unused vacation will
be paid out when the transfer happens. Your accrual rates for vacation or PTO
at your new organization will be based on your employment date with JHU.

4. Will I have to attend orientation again?

Yes, you will need to attend New-Hire Orientation at JHHS. We know you’ve been
through this before, but it’s important so you can get up to speed on the beneﬁts,
policies and compliance requirements speciﬁc to your new employer.

5. Will I have a probationary period?

Yes, all employees will have a 90-day probationary period as part of the
administrative transfer process. This is a standard organizational requirement, even
though your years of service with Johns Hopkins are being recognized for beneﬁts
purposes.

6. Who should I contact if I am interested in moving forward with retirement?
The JHU Benefits Office will host a retirement information session on June 16 at 11
a.m. Follow-up options to meet one-on-one with a benefits specialist will also be
available to you. Retirement planning resources are available at hr.jhu.edu/beneﬁts-
worklife/retirement.

1. Will there be training provided for new responsibilities or systems?

Absolutely. We will provide training and resources to help everyone succeed in their
roles, whether that’s learning new processes and systems or taking on different
responsibilities. No one will be expected to navigate these changes without support.

2. Will there be team-building opportunities after implementation to support the
new teams?

Yes. In addition to role and system training, we will offer team-building opportunities
after the transition is implemented to help teams gel, build trust and work effectively
together. These activities will be designed to reinforce collaboration, clarify team
norms and support ongoing development as teams settle into their new ways of
working.

3. How will I be supported during this transition?

We recognize that it takes time to process this information, and we’re here to support
you every step of the way. Don’t hesitate to reach out anytime if you’d like to
discuss this further.

If you’re feeling stressed, uncertain or simply need someone to talk to, there are
resources available to support you.

employees and their household family members. It is free, conﬁdential and

access to the help you need.
