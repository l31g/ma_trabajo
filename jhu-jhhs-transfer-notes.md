# JHU to JHHS Transfer Notes

## Context

My mom has worked at Johns Hopkins University (JHU) for about 18 years as a medical coder. Hopkins is transferring her department from JHU to Johns Hopkins Health System (JHHS), with the transfer expected around the end of August 2026.

Main concerns:

- Whether accrued PTO/vacation will be paid out at transfer time.
- Whether PTO resets at the end of the year.
- Whether she has a Hopkins pension, and whether the transfer affects it.

Most important confirmed policy points:

- **HR031 Vacation Leave**, effective **12/11/2025**, defines an **Administrative Transfer** as an involuntary movement from one organization's payroll to another, initiated by one of the covered organizations under JHU/JHHS employment-based criteria guidelines. It says the staff member is terminated from one organization and hired into the other payroll **with no loss of service**; **vacation leave is paid out** and **sick leave is transferred** to the receiving organization.
- HR031 also says individuals who administratively transfer to one of the eligible Johns Hopkins entities will be **paid out unused vacation leave at the time of the administrative transfer**, and their **University years of service will be used to calculate leave accrual** at the receiving organization.
- HR031 says staff hired or reinstated before **July 1, 2013** can receive pay for accrued unused vacation leave up to **44 days**. Any vacation leave above the vacation limit is forfeited at termination/retirement.
- **HR025 Sick Leave**, effective **12/11/2025**, uses the same Administrative Transfer definition and says **vacation leave is paid out and sick leave is transferred** to the receiving organization.
- HR025 says sick leave accrues up to **1 day per month** to a maximum of **90 days** for eligible staff, and individuals who administratively transfer to an eligible Johns Hopkins entity will have unused sick leave available at the time of transfer **transferred to the receiving organization**.
- **HR023 Position Categories**, effective **01/10/2022**, defines full-time as a regular position planned to last more than six months requiring at least **28 hours/week** or at least **75%** of full-time equivalent salary.
- **HR029 Transfers, Promotions, Demotions and Reclassifications**, effective **07/01/2017**, covers regular University job movement and says the University may initiate transfers, promotions, or demotions in response to organizational needs. The more specific JHU/JHHS administrative-transfer language is in HR031 and HR025.
- **HR026 Staff Compensation Program** covers salary ranges, promotion increases, demotions, equity adjustments, on-call pay, shift differential, acting pay, and discretionary bonus rules. It is useful for pay review but does not appear to answer PTO payout directly.

## PTO / vacation concern

The official JHU Vacation Leave policy now directly confirms the key PTO point. HR031 says an administrative transfer is a covered JHU/JHHS payroll movement with no loss of service, and that vacation leave is paid out while sick leave is transferred to the receiving organization.

- For a JHU-to-JHHS administrative transfer, unused accrued JHU vacation/PTO should be **paid out at the time of administrative transfer**.
- The PTO/vacation balance generally does **not** carry over to the receiving organization.
- The employee starts accruing time under the receiving organization's policies.
- The accrual rate at the receiving organization should be based on recognized University years of service.
- Sick leave should **transfer** to the receiving organization if this is an administrative transfer to an eligible Johns Hopkins entity.

For my mom, this means the key issue is whether her JHU accrued PTO/vacation should be paid out around the August transfer date, rather than waiting until year-end or resetting without payout.

If her materials say PTO will not be paid at transfer, or that it simply resets at year-end, that should be challenged/clarified in writing with HR because it appears inconsistent with HR031 Vacation Leave.

## Pension / retirement concern

There may be more than one relevant Hopkins retirement/pension plan:

### Possible JHU Support Staff Pension

Indexed summaries of JHU pension materials indicate:

- JHU had a traditional support staff pension plan for certain support staff.
- The plan was tied to older hire dates, commonly described as before July 1, 2009.
- Employees generally needed to meet eligibility and vesting requirements.
- Some eligible employees may have had to select or remain in the pension option during a retirement-choice period.

Because my mom has 18 years of service, she may fall into the relevant hire-date window, but this must be confirmed from her benefits records.

### Possible JHHS / JHHSC pension

Indexed summaries also indicate:

- JHHS/JHHSC has or had legacy pension coverage for certain employees.
- Some descriptions reference eligibility for non-represented staff hired before a cutoff around 2016.
- Newer employees are generally more likely to be under 403(b)-style retirement plans rather than a traditional defined-benefit pension.

### Transfer effect

A transfer should not erase an already vested pension benefit. However, the transfer could affect:

- Whether she continues earning pension service after the transfer.
- Whether pension accrual freezes at the transfer date.
- Whether future retirement benefits are under JHHS retirement/403(b) rules.
- Whether JHU service is recognized only for eligibility/vesting or also for benefit accrual.

The exact answer depends on the plan documents and her employee classification.
