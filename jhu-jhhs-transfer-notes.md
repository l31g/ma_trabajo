# JHU to JHHS Transfer Notes

## Context

My mom has worked at Johns Hopkins University (JHU) for about 18 years as a medical coder. Hopkins is transferring her department from JHU to Johns Hopkins Health System (JHHS), with the transfer expected around the end of August 2026.

Main concerns:

- Whether accrued PTO/vacation will be paid out at transfer time.
- Whether PTO resets at the end of the year.
- Whether she has a Hopkins pension, and whether the transfer affects it.

## Publicly confirmed source

The clearest accessible official source found was a Johns Hopkins Hub article:

- **Title:** "Transfer guidelines between JHU and JHHS have been expanded"
- **Published:** Jan. 16, 2026
- **URL:** https://hub.jhu.edu/at-work/2026/01/16/transfer-guidelines-between-jhu-and-jhhs-have-been-expanded/

Confirmed points from that article:

- JHU and JHHS are two separate organizations.
- Employee administrative transfer guidelines between JHU and JHHS were expanded.
- The guidelines cover benefits eligibility, transfer conditions, service dates, and more.
- All transfers between JHU and JHHS will be recognized, whether initiated by the organization or the employee.
- Years of service will transfer and be recognized across the organizations.
- The guidelines are effective Jan. 1, 2026.
- The guidelines are not retroactive.
- Questions should go to the divisional HR contact or the JHHS HR Support Center at 443-997-5400 or hrsc@jhmi.edu.

## PTO / vacation concern

The accessible official article confirms service recognition but does not include the detailed PTO rules. Direct access to the detailed HR PDFs was blocked from this environment, but indexed summaries of the official transfer-guideline PDFs consistently said:

- For a JHU-to-JHHS administrative transfer, unused accrued vacation/PTO is generally **paid out by the organization the employee is leaving**.
- The PTO/vacation balance generally does **not** carry over to the receiving organization.
- The employee starts accruing time under the receiving organization's policies.
- The accrual rate at the receiving organization should be based on the employee's recognized total years of service.
- Sick time may not transfer and may not be paid out.

For my mom, this means the key issue is whether her JHU accrued PTO/vacation should be paid out around the August transfer date, rather than waiting until year-end or resetting without payout.

If her materials say PTO will not be paid at transfer, or that it simply resets at year-end, that should be challenged/clarified in writing with HR because it appears inconsistent with indexed summaries of the JHU/JHHS administrative transfer guidance.

## Pension / retirement concern

There may be more than one relevant Hopkins retirement/pension plan:

### Possible JHU Support Staff Pension

Indexed summaries of JHU pension materials indicate:

- JHU had a traditional support staff pension plan for certain support staff.
- The plan was tied to older hire dates, commonly described as before July 1, 2009.
- Employees generally needed to meet eligibility and vesting requirements.
- Some eligible employees may have had to select or remain in the pension option during a retirement-choice period.

Because my mom has 18 years of service, she may fall into the relevant hire-date window, but this must be confirmed from her benefits records.

### Possible JHHS / JHHSC pension

Indexed summaries also indicate:

- JHHS/JHHSC has or had legacy pension coverage for certain employees.
- Some descriptions reference eligibility for non-represented staff hired before a cutoff around 2016.
- Newer employees are generally more likely to be under 403(b)-style retirement plans rather than a traditional defined-benefit pension.

### Transfer effect

A transfer should not erase an already vested pension benefit. However, the transfer could affect:

- Whether she continues earning pension service after the transfer.
- Whether pension accrual freezes at the transfer date.
- Whether future retirement benefits are under JHHS retirement/403(b) rules.
- Whether JHU service is recognized only for eligibility/vesting or also for benefit accrual.

The exact answer depends on the plan documents and her employee classification.

## Questions to ask HR in writing

1. Is this transfer being processed as a formal **JHU-to-JHHS administrative transfer** under the Jan. 1, 2026 guidelines?
2. What will my official JHHS service date be after transfer?
3. Will my 18 years of JHU service be recognized for PTO accrual rate, retirement eligibility, vesting, and other service-based benefits?
4. What is my current accrued JHU vacation/PTO balance?
5. Will my accrued JHU vacation/PTO be paid out at the August transfer date?
6. If PTO is not paid at transfer, what policy allows it to be delayed or reset?
7. Does any sick time transfer or get paid out?
8. Am I currently participating in a JHU pension plan, JHU 403(b), or both?
9. If I am in a JHU pension plan, will pension service continue accruing after the transfer to JHHS?
10. If pension accrual stops, what is the frozen/accrued benefit as of the transfer date?
11. What JHHS retirement plan will I be placed into after transfer?
12. Will JHU service count toward JHHS retirement eligibility, vesting, employer contributions, or pension service?
13. Can HR provide the specific plan document, summary plan description, or transfer-guideline section that governs the answer?

## Working interpretation

Based on public and indexed information, the likely position is:

- Her 18 years of service should be recognized across JHU and JHHS.
- Accrued JHU PTO/vacation likely should be paid out by JHU at or around the transfer.
- PTO balance likely does not simply carry into JHHS.
- Sick time may not transfer.
- A vested pension should not disappear, but future accrual depends on the plan and transfer terms.

This needs to be checked against the actual materials Hopkins gives her.
